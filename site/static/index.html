Welcome to Core Reflections

I am a freelance consultant who has set up Core Reflections a consultancy company which supports organisations with sustainable, value driven transformational changes. In addition to offering wellbeing services for staff groups and individuals and support services to organisations. I have been working with organisations to develop a shared core belief, be creative thinkers, develop business strategies and create shared values. This has in some cases led to a more entrepreneurial leadership which on evaluation has resulted in staff feeling more involved, appreciated and respected

I have 29 years of experience of working within the NHS in a Psychology/Counselling service in a Primary Care trust in East London where I was a Consultant Clinical Counsellor specialising in the physical and mental health fields. In 2013 the service became a Social Enterprise where I became one of the founding Directors, until 2018.

Before my NHS role I worked as a Youth and Community worker in supporting children, families and young people within their communities to thrive alongside mentoring young people to achieve their aspirations. I have been fortunate enough to have worked with a wide group of young people including young people at risk, youth offenders and supporting people in prisons. Deaf and disabled young adults.

I grew up in East London where I continue to live and work. I am passionate about challenging health and social inequalities.

My work practice demonstrates my belief in co-production. I have developed and delivered multiple services across health, and voluntary organisations within clinical and non-clinical services. Ensuring services are relevant, needed and respond to local need.

Core Reflections offers an accessible service including British Sign Language access to Deaf community members.

During the ongoing Coronavirus Core Reflections is responding by offering the following.

  • Supporting and consulting to Health Visiting Services in East London
  • Supporting and Consulting to School nursing services in East London
  • Supporting teachers dealing with Safeguarding issues during lockdown.
  • Supporting people with Farewell family /friends letters during the Coronavirus.
  • Free therapy for regular clients that have been affected by the Coronavirus, and have become unemployed.

My Ethos

Any work undertaken by Core Reflections is conducted with an ethos of respect, which is a belief I have carried with me throughout my working career.

This ethos represents the important idea that everyone has something important and valuable to offer, thus every person should be respected as such.

This encompasses the belief that services should be accessible for everyone and anything is possible if we support each other.

Testimonials

I have worked with Pauline as my one-to-one clinical supervisor for a number of years as part of my practice as a self-employed psychotherapist.

Quality of the supervision

Pauline is a very highly experienced therapist and supervisor and provides very high-quality supervision. She is highly reflective and is a very supportive presence. She has a particular strength in tuning in to the experience and needs of the clients I have discussed with her and is also very skilled in helping me to reflect on my own countertransference towards clients.

Professionalism

Pauline is very professional and is clear in her boundaries and agreements in relation to booking times, payments, managing confidentiality, etc.

Overall feeling of supervision.

Overall, my feeling is that Pauline is a very grounding and supportive supervisor. I am comfortable that she will treat any questions or uncertainties I might share with her without judgment and will work through any issues I may be struggling with as a colleague and peer. Coming away from a session with Pauline, I always feel as though I have had an opportunity to explore what I needed to in depth, allowing me to support my clients with more confidence. I also feel as though she has invested a great deal of reflection and consideration in understanding my strengths as a therapist and areas where I might benefit from guidance.

Support outside the clinical supervision sessions.

Pauline helpfully makes herself available in the case of any immediate pressing concerns or emergencies relating to client care. If I have an issue I would like to get guidance from her about at short notice, I know that I can contact her to organize a call and that she will find a space to speak to me as soon as possible.

John Pollard - Psychotherapist
